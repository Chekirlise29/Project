🔥 Anime Recommendations 🔥

Top Picks You Should Watch by Group 4!

This website showcases the hobbies of group 4, specifically focusing on anime. Here, we present the types of anime we recommend along with our thoughts on each.

Yosuga No Sora

Genre: Drama, Romance, Seinen

Content Advisory: 18+, Nudity, Profanity, Drama, Romance, Ecchi

After losing both of their parents in an accident, Haruka and his twin sister Sora move out of the city to the rural town where they spent their childhood summers. In their new home, everything seems peaceful until Haruka discovers a special, forbidden love that may disrupt the entire town!

Umamusume

Genre: Sports

Content Advisory: PG-13 - Teens 13 or older

Special Week is a horse girl who wants to be the best racehorse in Japan. She comes to Tokyo to attend the Tracen Academy, which trains young horsegirls like her to become better racers. Special Week is motivated to become a racer after witnessing Silence Suzuka's elegant running technique there. Soon after, Special Week is enlisted in Spica, Silence Suzuka's team. One lap at a time, she then starts her journey to the summit.

Your Name

Genre: Drama, Supernatural

Content Advisory: PG-13 - Teens 13 or older

Two teenagers share a profound, magical connection upon discovering they are swapping bodies. Things manage to become even more complicated when the boy and girl decide to meet in person.

Maquia: When the Promised Flower Blooms

Genre: Drama, Fantasy

Content Advisory: PG-13 - Teens 13 or older

Maquia belongs to the Iorph, a mystical race that lives for hundreds of years. Feeling lonely and curious about the outside world, her life changes when the kingdom of Mezarte attacks her homeland to gain the Iorph’s immortality. Separated and left alone after the destruction, Maquia discovers an abandoned human baby and decides to raise him as her own, despite the challenges of caring for a child who will grow old while she remains the same.

Your lie in April

Genre: Shounen, Drama, Music

Content Advisory: PG-13 - Teens 13 or older

Kousei Arima was a genius pianist until his mother’s sudden death took away his ability to play. Each day was monotone for Kousei, but when he meets a violinist named Kaori Miyazono who has an eccentric playing style, his monotonous life begins to take on color.

⭐ Group 4 Reviews ⭐

Yosuga No Sora

Yosuga no Sora is an anime show about two siblings, a brother and a sister, who do questionable things because their relationship goes far beyond that of normal siblings. It becomes much more intimate, almost like they are truly real lovers. But that's just the tip of the iceberg, I highly recommend checking out the anime and see how everything develops between the two siblings. 10/10 anime that I would watch it again, though I do not recommend watching this with your parents, but watching this with your sibling, especially if you have a sister, is definitely recommended!

Umamusume

If you're into horse racing but imagine it with a twist of anime girls? then this show is perfect for you. Yes, this show is literally just the same as in real-life horse racing, but instead it's the anime girls doing the race as the concept of this anime. Looking for a good story-wise that will keep you engaged and get you on edge every race? Then this anime is what you're looking for, as they have tons of seasons and spin-offs, so you won't get bored once you get hooked on this. It even has a game if you're interested in checking that one out.

Your Name

Honestly, Your Name just hits different. It starts off as this cute, funny body-swap story, but then it completely pivots and rips your heart out in the best way possible. The visuals are absolutely insane—like, every single frame could be a wallpaper—but it’s the constant feeling of "searching for something" that really gets to you. It perfectly captures that weird, bittersweet ache of missing someone you haven't even met yet. If you're looking for a movie that makes you feel small but hopeful at the same time, this is 100% it.

Maquia

When the Promised Flower Blooms is a beautifully animated fantasy drama that tells a heartfelt story about motherhood and the passage of time. The film follows Maquia, a girl from a long-lived race, as she raises a human child in a world filled with war and loss. Its emotional depth, stunning visuals, and touching soundtrack create a powerful viewing experience. Overall, it is a moving and unforgettable film that gently explores love, sacrifice, and the pain of letting go.

Your Lie in April

Your Lie in April is a beautiful, colorful gut punch. It’s not just about classical music, it’s about how one person can crash into your life and force you to see the world in color again, even when you’re drowning in your own head. Watching Kousei and Kaori’s relationship unfold is honestly exhausting in the best way possible—it’s hopeful, vibrant, and then suddenly, deeply tragic. It’s one of those shows that stays with you forever, mostly because it captures that raw, messy feeling of your first big love and your first big heartbreak all at once. Just a fair warning: that final letter will absolutely wreck you.
